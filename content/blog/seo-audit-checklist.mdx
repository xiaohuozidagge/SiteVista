Running a website without regular SEO audits is like driving without ever checking your dashboard. You might be cruising along just fine--or you might have warning lights flashing that you cannot see. A thorough SEO audit reveals exactly where your website stands and what needs attention.

This checklist walks through every major component of a modern SEO audit. Use it as your reference whether you are auditing your own site or preparing to work with a professional.

## Technical SEO Checklist

Technical SEO forms the foundation of your website's search performance. Without a crawlable, indexable site, even the best content goes unseen.

**Crawlability checks:**
- Verify Google Search Console is properly configured and receiving data.
- Crawl the site with a tool like Screaming Frog or Sitebulb to identify broken links, redirect chains, and orphan pages.
- Review robots.txt to confirm it is not accidentally blocking important sections.
- Check XML sitemap structure and submission status in Search Console.
- Ensure all important pages return a 200 status code and not soft 404s.

**Indexability checks:**
- Audit for unintentional noindex tags on pages that should be indexed.
- Review canonical tags for correctness and consistency.
- Check pagination handling and parameter-based URL management.
- Identify duplicate content or near-duplicate pages that may waste crawl budget.
- Verify that the correct version of the site (www vs non-www, HTTP vs HTTPS) is canonicalized.

**Performance fundamentals:**
- Run key pages through PageSpeed Insights to capture Core Web Vitals scores.
- Audit image file sizes and formats--large uncompressed images are a common drag on load time.
- Check for render-blocking JavaScript and CSS that delays first paint.
- Verify mobile responsiveness across all critical templates.

## On-Page SEO Checklist

On-page SEO is where content meets structure. Each page needs to signal its topic clearly to both search engines and users.

**Title tags and meta descriptions:**
- Every indexable page must have a unique, descriptive title tag within the recommended character limit.
- Meta descriptions should be compelling summaries that encourage click-through, even though they are not a direct ranking factor.
- Check for missing, duplicate, or truncated title tags across the site.

**Heading structure:**
- Each page should have exactly one H1 tag that clearly states the primary topic.
- Subheadings should follow a logical hierarchy without skipping levels.
- Headings should include relevant terms naturally, not stuffed with keywords.

**Content and keyword placement:**
- Primary keywords should appear in the first paragraph, at least one H2, and naturally throughout the body.
- Image alt text should describe the image accurately and include keywords where relevant.
- URL slugs should be concise, readable, and include the primary keyword.

## Content Audit Checklist

Content is the reason people visit your site. A content audit determines whether your pages are actually serving user needs.

**Content inventory:**
- Catalog all pages and group by content type--blog posts, service pages, product pages, landing pages.
- Record traffic, rankings, and conversions for each page where data is available.
- Flag pages with thin content, defined as fewer than 300 words on informational pages.

**Quality assessment:**
- Check for outdated statistics, references, or advice that undermines credibility.
- Identify pages that target the same keyword and may be cannibalizing each other.
- Evaluate whether each page satisfies the search intent behind its target keyword.
- Review for proper E-E-A-T signals: author credentials, citations, recent publication dates.

**Consolidation decisions:**
- For underperforming pages, choose between improving content, merging with a stronger page, or implementing a 301 redirect.
- Prune content that has no traffic, no backlinks, and no realistic path to ranking.

## Off-Page SEO Checklist

Off-page factors, primarily backlinks, remain one of the strongest ranking signals. An audit should evaluate both quantity and quality.

**Backlink profile review:**
- Audit the backlink profile for toxic or spammy links that could trigger a penalty.
- Identify high-quality referring domains and look for patterns in what attracts links.
- Check for broken backlinks pointing to pages that no longer exist and implement redirects.
- Assess the anchor text distribution--too many exact-match anchors can look manipulative.

**Competitive link gap:**
- Compare your backlink profile against top-ranking competitors to find link-building opportunities.
- Identify unlinked brand mentions that could be converted to links.

A systematic SEO audit using this checklist will surface most issues holding a website back. Prioritize fixes by impact and effort, address technical problems first, and revisit the checklist quarterly to stay ahead of new issues.
