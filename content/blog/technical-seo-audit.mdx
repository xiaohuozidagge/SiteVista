Technical SEO is the engine room of your website's search performance. If search engines cannot crawl, understand, or index your pages efficiently, your content and backlinks matter far less. A technical SEO audit systematically tests every layer of your site's infrastructure to find and fix issues before they become ranking problems.

This guide covers the five pillars of a thorough technical SEO audit: crawlability, indexing, site architecture, Core Web Vitals, and structured data.

## Crawlability

Crawlability determines whether search engine bots can access and navigate your site. The first step in any technical audit is verifying that crawlers are not being blocked or misled.

A crawl audit begins with reviewing your robots.txt file. This small text file instructs bots which sections of your site to avoid. One misplaced directive can deindex an entire directory. After verifying robots.txt, run a full crawl with a desktop tool like Screaming Frog to map every URL the bot discovers.

Look for broken internal links that send crawlers to 404 pages, redirect chains that waste crawl budget, and orphan pages--URLs that exist in your sitemap but have no internal links pointing to them. Each broken link is a dead end for both crawlers and users.

JavaScript rendering is another critical crawlability concern. If your navigation or key content relies on client-side JavaScript, verify that Google can render and discover those elements. Server-side rendering or dynamic rendering may be necessary for JavaScript-heavy sites.

## Indexing

Once pages are crawled, they must be indexed to appear in search results. An indexing audit checks which pages are actually in Google's index and why certain pages are excluded.

Google Search Console's Index Coverage report is your primary tool here. It categorizes excluded pages by reason: crawled but not indexed, discovered but not crawled, duplicate without user-selected canonical, and pages blocked by robots.txt among others.

Review your XML sitemaps to confirm they include only indexable, canonical URLs. Sitemaps that reference redirected, noindexed, or non-canonical pages send confusing signals. Check that pagination is handled correctly with proper rel next and prev tags, and that faceted navigation parameters are managed through URL parameter tools in Search Console.

Duplicate content is an indexing efficiency killer. Audit canonical tags to ensure every page points to its preferred version. Look for duplicate or near-duplicate pages generated by session IDs, tracking parameters, or printer-friendly versions of pages.

## Site Architecture

Site architecture shapes how link equity flows through your domain and how easily users navigate your content. A flat, logical architecture distributes authority more effectively than deep, siloed structures.

Audit your site's information hierarchy by examining click depth. Critical pages should be reachable within three clicks from the homepage. Pages buried deeper receive less internal link equity and are less likely to rank well.

Internal linking is the connective tissue of good architecture. Audit anchor text variety, identify pages that could benefit from more internal links, and check that your navigation menus surface the most important sections. A well-linked site helps both users and crawlers discover related content.

URL structure also matters. Clean, descriptive URLs that include relevant keywords perform better than parameter-heavy strings of numbers and characters. Audit for consistency in URL patterns, especially on e-commerce sites where product categories generate complex URL structures.

## Core Web Vitals

Core Web Vitals became a formal ranking signal in 2021, and they remain a critical part of any technical audit. These metrics measure real-world user experience: loading performance, interactivity, and visual stability.

Largest Contentful Paint measures how quickly the main content loads. Audit for opportunities in image optimization, server response time, and render-blocking resources. Cumulative Layout Shift captures visual stability--pages where content jumps around as elements load create a frustrating user experience and score poorly. Interaction to Next Paint evaluates responsiveness to user input.

Use PageSpeed Insights and the Chrome User Experience Report to gather field data. Lab data from Lighthouse is useful for debugging but field data reflects what real users actually experience across different devices and network conditions.

## Structured Data

Structured data helps search engines understand your content and enables rich results like review stars, recipe cards, and FAQ accordions in search listings.

Audit your site for structured data implementation using Google's Rich Results Test and Schema Markup Validator. Check that each page type uses the appropriate schema type: Article for blog posts, Product for e-commerce pages, LocalBusiness for location pages, and so on.

Look for errors, warnings, and missing required properties. Even valid structured data with warnings may not qualify for rich results. Also check for opportunities where structured data could unlock new search features--FAQ schema on appropriate pages, HowTo schema for instructional content, and BreadcrumbList schema for navigation paths.

A comprehensive technical SEO audit ties all five pillars together into a prioritized action plan. Fix crawlability and indexing issues first, since they gate everything else. Then address architecture and performance, and finally enrich your pages with structured data to earn richer SERP real estate.
