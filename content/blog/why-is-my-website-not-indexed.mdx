## Common Causes

### Robots.txt Blocking

Check your `robots.txt` file. A single misplaced `Disallow: /` can prevent your entire site from being indexed.

### Noindex Tags

Audit your pages for accidental `noindex` meta tags. These can be left over from development environments.

### Crawl Budget Issues

Large sites may have crawl budget problems where Google does not crawl all pages frequently enough.

### Low-Quality Content

Google may choose not to index pages with thin or duplicate content.

## How to Fix It

1. Check Google Search Console coverage reports
2. Verify robots.txt and meta robots tags
3. Submit a sitemap
4. Use the URL Inspection tool to request indexing
